# Security Policy

We take the security of Lionade and the safety of our users' data seriously, and we welcome good-faith security research.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Email **security@getlionade.com** with the details. If that address bounces, use **support@getlionade.com** and put "security" in the subject.

A useful report includes:

- A clear description of the issue and its impact.
- The affected URL, endpoint, or screen.
- Step-by-step instructions to reproduce it, and a proof of concept if you have one.
- Any accounts, tools, or conditions needed to trigger it.

The machine-readable contact is published at [`/.well-known/security.txt`](https://getlionade.com/.well-known/security.txt), and the full policy (scope, out of scope, safe harbor, and what to expect) lives at <https://getlionade.com/security>.

## Scope

In scope: the Lionade web app at getlionade.com and its API, the Lionade iOS app, and the controls around authentication, the Fang economy and payouts, payments, and the AI features.

Out of scope: third-party platforms we build on (Supabase, Stripe, Vercel, Apple), volumetric denial of service, social engineering, and scanner output with no validated finding behind it. See the full policy for the complete list.

## Safe harbor

If you make a good-faith effort to follow our policy while researching, we will treat your research as authorized, we will not pursue or support legal action against you for it, and we will work with you to resolve the issue quickly. To the extent your research complies with this policy, we consider it authorized under applicable computer-crime law (including the CFAA and DMCA Section 1201) and not a breach of our Terms of Service. Please only test accounts you control, and never access, collect, or retain another user's personal data. Our users include minors, whose data is categorically off limits: capture only the minimum proof needed, stop, report, and delete any copy once we confirm receipt. Do not degrade the service, and give us a reasonable chance to fix an issue before disclosing it publicly.

## What to expect

We acknowledge valid reports within a few business days, keep you updated through the fix, and credit you publicly once it is resolved, with your permission. We do not run a paid bug-bounty program yet, but valid reports earn public acknowledgment.
